Spark + Symphony


How we use information


Spark + Symphony is a limited company registered in England and Wales. We run this website (www.sparkandsymphony.com) and its subdomains.
We use third party services to keep in contact with people, do our work and share it. We also use it to understand how we can do all of those things better.
We’re happy to provide any further information or explanation if needed. If you’d like further explanation or have any questions, feel free to:

  • email data@sparkandsymphony.com
  • or write to Spark + Symphony, 86 – 90 Paul Street, London, EC2A 4NE
This page was last updated on 18 January 2021.

Our websites

What information we collect

We collect the following information about people who visit our websites:

  • IP address (first two bytes)
  • Timestamp of visit
  • Country of visit
  • Referrer
  • Operating system
  • Browser
  • Screen resolution
  • Device type
  • Device brand and model
  • Pages visited during session

Why we collect website information

We collect analytics on our website to see how people use it and it allows us to improve their experience.

How long we keep website information

We anonymise the information we collect and store it indefinitely. This help us to see how people use the websites over time and how it has changed.

Where website information is processed and stored

We use an analytics software called Google analytics. We use it to help us understand how people are using our website so we can make a better experience for them. We do not make and do not allow Google to make, any attempt to find out the identities of those visiting our website. If we do want to collect personally identifiable information through our website, we will be upfront about this and ask for your explicit consent. We will make it clear when we collect personal information and will explain what we intend to do with it.

Opt-out of collection

You can opt out of our analytics by turning ‘Do Not Track’ on in your browser. You can do this in Google Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Internet Explorer and Microsoft Edge.

Social media

We use LinkedIn to share our work. We sometimes use the analytic tools provided by LinkedIn to understand how people are interacting with our work shared on it. You can find out the privacy policy for LinkedIn here.

If you’d like content about your removed from this site or on LinkedIn, please email data@sparkandsymphony.com.

Research Participants

When we work organisations to build better products and services to empower people and communities using it, research is a key part of our work. It helps us understand people’s needs and make sure that the products and services we build meets those needs and solve their problems.

What information we collect

We collect the following information from research participants:

Participant information
  • Full name
  • Telephone number
  • Email address
  • Address
Consent forms
  • Full name
  • Signature
Research material
  • Interview recordings (audio or screen recordings)
  • Quotes
  • Notes
  • Photos

Potential and existing clients

What information we collect

We may collect the following information about our potential and existing clients:

  • Name
  • Company
  • Email address
  • Phone number
  • Job title
  • Company address
  • Working hours
  • Testimonials

Why we collect this information

We use this information to create and manage client relationships. We may use testimonials on our websites to show what it’s like working with us.

How long we keep this information

We keep information about potential clients for 6 months from last contact and information about existing clients for 2 years from last contact. Testimonials are kept for 5 years.

Where data about potential and existing clients is processed

We use the following services to store and process this data:

  • GSuite, including Gmail and Drive
  • Notion
  • Miro

Things we don’t do

Spark and symphony doesn’t take part in the following activities:

  • Buying or selling marketing lists
  • Entering into data sharing agreements with other organisations
  • Telephone marketing
  • Postal marketing
  • CCTV surveillance (apart from CCTV systems run by the buildings in which we work)
We don’t use “soft opt-in”, meaning you won’t receive any marketing communications from us unless you’ve specifically agreed to it.

Keeping information secure

We carefully choose our services and tools at Spark + Symphony. It’s important that they follow good security practices, like HTTPS, two-factor authentication and the ability to set a strong password. We’ve reviewed the privacy policies and security practices of everything we use. When a new team member joins Spark + Symphony, we explain best practices for keeping their devices secure, maintaining the security of their online accounts and working outside our offices.

Information breaches

In the event of an information breach, we are required to notify the Information Commissioner’s Office. We will do so following their guidance.

Exemptions

There are exemptions to data protection regulations that may require us to share information about you, including requests by law enforcement. A full list of exemptions are listed on the ICO website – this also applies to information held about you by third party services we use.

Reviewing how we use information

Every 6 months, we review the third party services as well as the way we collect and use information. This helps us to hold ourselves to account and it helps us improve our ways of working. We will update this page as necessary.

Your rights and getting in touch

The UK GDPR provides the following rights:

  • Right to be informed
  • Right of access
  • Right to rectification
  • Right to erasure
  • Right to restrict processing
  • Right to data portability
  • Right to object
  • Rights related to automated decision-making, including profiling

To exercise any of these rights, you can email us at data@sparkandsymphony.com or write to us at Spark + Symphony, 86 – 90 Paul Street, London, EC2A 4NE. In order to fulfil the request, we must be able to verify your identity and may ask for further details about you and your contact with Spark + Symphony. We will respond to a request within one calendar month of receiving it, free of charge. However, if the request is complex or there are numerous requests we may extend this deadline by a further two months and/or charge a fee, we will notify you if this is the case and explain why. You can find information specific to the services we use or our activities in the relevant sections of this page. If you aren’t satisfied by our response, you can contact the Information Commissioner’s Office.

Complaints or queries

Privacy is a right not a luxury so for this reason, Spark + Symphony tries to meet highest possible standard when collecting and using personal information. We take any complaints seriously. We encourage people to raise it to our attention if they think that our collection or use of information is unfair, misleading, or inappropriate. You will not get into trouble; it helps us be better in how we work and keep you safe.
